Mahatma Gandhi

Biography

Early Life and Education

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, also known as Mahatma Gandhi, was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, a coastal town in present-day Gujurat, India. He was born into a Hindu merchant caste family and was the youngest of four children.

Education

Gandhi was a good student and completed his early education in India. He then went to London to study law and was called to the bar in 1891. He returned to India and set up a law practice in Bombay, but he was not successful.

The Fight for India Independence

Gandhi became involved in the Indian independence movement in 1915, after he was able to lead the Indian National Congress. He became known for his philosophy of nonviolent resistance, which he called Satyagraha. Gandhi led several nonviolent campaigns and protests against British rule, including the Salt March in 1930 and the Quit India Movement in 1942.

Imprisonment

Gandhi was arrested several times for his involvment in the independence movement and spent a total of about seven years in prison. While in prison, he continued to write and advocate for nonviolent resistance.

Later Life and Legacy

After India gained independence in 1947, Gandhi continued to work for peace and reconciliation between Hindus and Muslims. However, he was assassinated on January 30, 1948, by a Hindu natinalist who opposed his views on religious tolerance.

Legacy

Gandhi is remembered today as one of the most influential leaders in the history of India and as a champion of nonviolent resistance. His philosophy of Satyagraha inspired other leaders, including Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela.
